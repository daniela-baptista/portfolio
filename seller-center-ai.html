The scale problem

Seller support was handling a large volume of repetitive operational queries, many of which did not require human intervention.

The challenge was to improve autonomy, reduce ticket dependency and make support available beyond business hours.

The analysis

I mapped the most recurrent ticket themes and identified clear behavioural patterns that could be solved through guided self-service.

Existing documentation from Seller Academy and the ticket workflow system became the knowledge foundation for the AI assistant.

What was built

Built the business case and defined the support opportunity, including issue mapping, prioritisation and solution framework.

Worked on knowledge base structuring, content optimisation, validation flows and live support logic for PT and ES.

Jira Confluence Seller Center Seller Academy Azure Mirakl

Product Thinking

Beyond the chatbot itself, the project involved defining intents, fallback logic, escalation pathways and content governance.

This was designed as a scalable product layer rather than a one-off support feature.

Live implementation

Live seller-facing chatbot inside Seller Center.
Support data and operational analysis feeding the business case.

Key learning

The quality of AI depends on the quality of the underlying information architecture.

Clean documentation, clear issue categories and scalable workflows were critical to making the chatbot useful in production.

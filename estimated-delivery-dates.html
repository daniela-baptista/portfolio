Estimated Delivery Dates

Redefining post-purchase delivery expectations through data-driven journey analysis.

Key Insight

65% of “late” orders were actually within SLA

The core issue was not logistics performance, but a mismatch between customer expectations and how delivery timelines were communicated.

The Problem

Customer dissatisfaction around delivery timelines was one of the main drivers of post-purchase support contacts and negative NSS feedback. Initial assumptions pointed toward logistics delays.

However, deeper analysis revealed that the main issue was not delivery performance itself, but the way estimated dates were presented across the customer journey.

My Approach

  • Analysed 346,000 shipment records
  • Reviewed NSS complaint themes and support tickets
  • Cross-checked SLA vs perceived delay
  • Mapped customer expectation touchpoints
  • Identified gaps in ETA communication logic

Solution Design

I proposed improvements to the estimated delivery communication flow, focusing on expectation setting and timeline clarity.

Before

Generic delivery windows and limited visibility led customers to perceive compliant orders as delayed.

After

Clearer ETA messaging, better range explanation and improved milestone communication reduced confusion.

Business Impact

The project shifted the focus from operational blame to expectation management, enabling more accurate root-cause analysis and better communication strategies.

This created the basis for reducing repetitive support contacts and improving post-purchase customer trust.

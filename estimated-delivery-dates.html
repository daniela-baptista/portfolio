Estimated Delivery Dates

Customer journey initiative focused on improving delivery expectation clarity and reducing dissatisfaction caused by false delivery perceptions.

Delivery journey visual / checkout flow coming soon

Problem

Customers had limited visibility on when their orders would arrive. Before the initiative, the Product Detail Page only displayed broad estimates such as “one week”, creating false expectations and increasing dissatisfaction.

Action

Introduced estimated delivery dates across key customer touchpoints: Product Detail Page, checkout and transactional emails, creating a more transparent and consistent journey.

My role

Led the initial analysis and identified the customer expectation gap. Worked cross-functionally with customer experience, platform and brand teams to align the solution with Worten guidelines and operational constraints.

Impact

Reduced false expectations around delivery timing and supported customer satisfaction improvements through increased transparency. The initiative was designed to positively influence NSS and NPS.

The delivery problem that wasn’t about delivery

Redefining delivery estimates to reduce customer uncertainty and align expectations with operational reality.

The Challenge

Customers frequently perceived orders as delayed, even when operations were performing within SLA. The issue was not delivery speed itself, but the gap between communication and expectation.

Diagnostic Process

I analysed support contacts, shipment records and key journey touchpoints to validate whether the problem was operational or perceptual.

The evidence showed that the friction was expectation-based.

Data & Findings

65% of orders customers flagged as late were within the expected delivery window. The real issue was the gap between what we communicated and what customers expected.

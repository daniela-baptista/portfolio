Building a support system from scratch

A workflow design project focused on creating structure, visibility and scalability for seller support operations.

Challenge

The support operation lacked documentation, standardised flows and clear ownership. Tickets were being handled inconsistently, creating delays, duplicated work and knowledge dependency on specific team members.

Operational Pain Points

0

documented process flows

High

knowledge dependency risk

Low

visibility over ticket status

Solution Design

I mapped the end-to-end ticket lifecycle, defined ownership stages, created categorisation logic and escalation rules, and introduced a standard workflow that could be replicated across teams.

1. Intake & Categorisation

Defined ticket entry points, issue types and priority logic.

2. Routing & Ownership

Established clear responsibilities and handoff rules.

3. Resolution & Documentation

Created reusable resolution flows and knowledge base standards.

Before vs After

Before

Process knowledge lived in people’s heads and operational continuity was highly vulnerable to absence.

After

A scalable workflow system reduced dependency, improved consistency and accelerated onboarding.

Impact

This project transformed support from an informal task flow into an operational framework with clear governance, visibility and scalability.
